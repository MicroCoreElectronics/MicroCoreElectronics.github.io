### Services List

#### 1. Product Specification & Requirements
Before a single trace is drawn, we define exactly what your product needs to do. I work with you to document electrical requirements, system architecture, power budgets, interface specifications, and component strategy — setting a clear foundation that prevents costly redesigns down the road.

#### 2. Schematic Design
Full schematic capture for any level of complexity — microcontrollers, power management, RF front-ends, sensor interfaces, communication buses (UART, SPI, I2C, CAN), and more. Clean, readable, and well-documented.

#### 3. PCB Layout & Design
Multi-layer PCB design with professional attention to signal integrity, EMC best practices, thermal management, and Design for Manufacturability (DFM). Whether it's a compact IoT node or a dense RF board, layout is done right.

#### 4. Prototype Development
I coordinate the full prototype build — sourcing components, generating manufacturing files, and managing assembly. When the board arrives, I bring it up systematically and get it functional.

#### 5. Testing & Debugging
Bring-up, functional testing, and root cause analysis. I use oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers, network analyzers, and custom test setups to find what's wrong and fix it.

#### 6. Manufacturing Support & Production Files
Everything your manufacturer needs: Gerber files, drill files, BOM, pick-and-place (centroid) files, assembly drawings, and test specifications. I can also coordinate directly with PCB fabs and PCBA houses.

#### 7. Full Product Development — Concept to Production
The complete package. You come with an idea or a problem. I deliver a validated, manufacturable product. This includes all stages above, plus ongoing design iteration, revision management, and production readiness review.

## PAGE 4: ABOUT

### Title: The Engineer Behind MicroCore

### Main Text:
I'm a hardware electronics engineer with 5+ years of experience taking products from concept all the way to production.

My background spans IoT systems, RF design, power electronics, embedded hardware, motor control, sensor interfaces, and wireless communication — essentially the full breadth of modern hardware design.

I started MicroCore Electronics because I kept seeing the same problem: great ideas stuck in development hell because the engineering process was fragmented. Designer hands off to prototyper, prototyper hands off to manufacturer, and somewhere in the middle, things break down.

My approach is different. I stay with the project from the first whiteboard sketch to the final production run. That means fewer handoffs, fewer surprises, and a product that actually gets to market.

**What I work with:**
- Microcontrollers & SoCs (ARM Cortex-M, ESP32, Nordic nRF series, and more)
- RF & Wireless (BLE, Wi-Fi, LoRa, Zigbee, cellular, custom RF)
- Power electronics (SMPS, LDO, battery management, high-current design)
- Sensors & interfaces (IMU, environmental, optical, industrial)
- Communication protocols (UART, SPI, I2C, CAN, Ethernet, USB)
- PCB design tools: Altium Designer, KiCad
- Test & measurement: oscilloscope, spectrum analyzer, network analyzer, DMM
